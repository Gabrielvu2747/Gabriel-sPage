Symphonie Espagnole 1st Movement by Edward Lalo 04/27/22

This piece, written in 1874 by Edward Lalo, specifically for the violinist Pablo Sarasate, who published Intro and Tarentella, a piece I learned in 2021, is known as a "Spanish Symphony." Although many consider it a violin concerto today, it was designed for orchestra. This is only the first movement out of the 5 movements that exist. It is one of 2 of Lalo's most often played works. The other one is the Cello Concerto in D minor.

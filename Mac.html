iMac (desktop computer)

The iMac is one of the computers that is made by Apple Inc. It is available as a desktop computer and as a laptop, however, the laptop variant is known as the MacBook. Originally, it was built in 1998 with an egg-shaped look. Later on, they changed it to a more professional design, with a stand that can move the monitor back and forth.

In 2007, the iMac underwent a redesign, sharing the same form as the previous, but they removed the SuperDrive, an optical disk drive that was made by Apple Inc. It even uses a different display unit, with a glass panel protecting the screen. This included faster graphics, better SSD options, and even some wifi cards to be included.

In 2014, another redesign occurred to the Mac, which had a Retina 5K monitor at a resolution of 5120x2880 pixels. A new processor and a graphics chip were included with new storage options. The newer I/O system was introduced on this Mac, with its screen being 27 inches wide. They even made another revision of the Mac, which is 21.5 inches wide in screen.

In 2017, the iMac Pro, the workstation version of the iMac to be built, features Xeon processors. It is colored in gray rather than silver, which got discontinued 4 years later. That was when the 24 inch iMac was built, with 7 different colors with a 4.5K retina display. These 7 colors were silver, blue, green, orange, yellow, purple, and pink. These supply the M1 chipset nowadays with Thunderbolt/USB 4 ports, with 2 USB ports.

MacBook

The MacBook is a laptop that replaced the iBook and PowerBook computers. The Pro versions exist from 2006-present, while the Air was made since 2008. It is made out of aluminum that was first used in the MacBook Air. It has black keys on its keyboard, where its original line was discontinued from 2012-15. It was discontinued again in July 2019 for the 3rd gen MacBook Air.
